Your data belongs to you. Flowstep does not use your personal data to train AI models, and the large language model (LLM) providers that power Flowstep's AI features are contractually prohibited from using your data for training purposes either. This applies to everything you create, upload, or interact with inside Flowstep.

## What Flowstep does not do

- Flowstep does **not** feed your designs, prompts, or project content into AI model training.
- Flowstep's LLM providers are **not permitted** to use your data to train or fine-tune their models.

## Aggregated and anonymized feedback

<Note>
  Flowstep may use aggregated, anonymized feedback to improve the quality of AI outputs. This data cannot be traced back to you or your projects — it contains no personally identifiable information and no raw content from your work.
</Note>

This limited use of anonymous feedback helps Flowstep make its AI features more accurate and useful for everyone, without compromising your privacy.

## If the approach changes

If Flowstep's data practices ever change, you will know about it. Flowstep will update its Privacy Policy and notify users before any new data uses take effect. You won't be surprised by changes to how your data is handled.

## Questions about privacy

<Tip>
  If you have questions about how Flowstep handles your data, reach out to the team at [hello@flowstep.ai](mailto:hello@flowstep.ai). You can also review the full Flowstep Privacy Policy at [flowstep.ai](https://flowstep.ai) for complete details.
</Tip>
